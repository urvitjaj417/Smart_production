Applicable Standards Reference
AMS 2430 — Aerospace Shot Peening
Scope: Shot peening of metallic parts (aerospace)
Intensity: Almen A or C strip; arc height per drawing
Coverage: Min 100% unless otherwise specified
Media: SAE J444 steel shot or conditioned cut wire
Saturation: 2T test mandatory. Δarc < 10%
Records: Full traceability — strip, operator, lot
MIL-S-13165C — Military / Defence
Scope: Shot peening for fatigue life improvement
Intensity: Almen A strip; 0.006–0.024 inch arc height
Coverage: 100% min (visual or fluorescent method)
Media: Steel shot per MIL-S-851; glass bead per MIL-G-9954
Saturation: Required per test coupon
Frequency: Each setup and 4-hour production intervals
SAE J2597 — Automotive Peening
Scope: Shot peening of springs, gears, shafts
Intensity: Almen A; typically 0.15–0.40 A
Coverage: 80–100% typical; per part drawing
Media: SAE J444; conditioned cut wire (SAE J1830)
Key metric: Fatigue life improvement ≥ 2× baseline
ISO 26910 — International Standard
Scope: Springs — shot peening specification
Intensity: Almen A or N; per design drawing
Coverage: 98–100% minimum
Verification: 3-point Almen test per setup
Records: Retained min 10 years

How Planning Data Improves Accuracy
  • ✓Pre-validates specs before the job runs — each planned job's intensity/coverage/pressure target is checked against the chosen standard automatically, flagging issues before media is loaded.
  • ✓Auto-fills the Spec Checker — clicking "Check" on a planned row loads its parameters straight into the Spec Checker tab.
  • ✓Feeds the Job Log — completed planned jobs move into the Job Log with PASS/FAIL based on actual measured intensity vs the plan's spec.
  • ✓Drives Overview KPIs — "Parts Today" target and queue count update live from the planning queue, instead of a fixed number.
